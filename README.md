# The Unlucky Astronaut

Group Number: 3B

## Description

**Game Concept**

Travel through the solar system and explore the cosmos. But hurry—your spacesuit has a leak, and your oxygen is running out. Dodge obstacles that drain your air, avoid the mysterious stardust, and reach the base before it’s too late!

**Gameplay Mechanics**

- The astronaut automatically moves forward.
- Your position on the X-axis remains fixed.
- You control movement on the Y-axis only.
- Air supply constantly decreases over time.
- Hitting an obstacle reduces your remaining air supply.
- Collecting stardust triggers a negative effect on the player for the rest of the level.
- Reach the base before your oxygen runs out to complete the mission.

**Player Experience**

Players experience a constant sense of urgency as their air supply steadily drains, creating pressure to keep moving while carefully navigating obstacles. The simple controls allow them to quickly enter a state of flow, but unpredictable stardust effects force them to adapt their strategy and remain alert. Moments of relief after successfully avoiding hazards are contrasted with the frustration and uncertainty of suddenly becoming slowed or disoriented by the stardust. As the level progresses and the number of obstacles increases, players become more immersed in the astronaut's struggle, in turn fostering empathy for the unpredictable and uncontrollable challenges that people living with MCS can experience.

## Design Rationale

Level 1 is designed so players can quickly understand the game through clear visual cues and gradual learning. Before gameplay begins, Mission Control introduces the controls and core mechanics one instruction at a time, teaching players how to fly, avoid hazards, and monitor their air supply, progress, and suit status. During gameplay, the astronaut remains fixed on the left side of the screen and always faces right, naturally indicating the direction of travel. Obstacles move toward the player, encouraging them to dodge the incoming hazards, while the Air Supply meter and Distance to Target display provide consistent feedback about progress and urgency.

GameFlow principles are supported by introducing mechanics progressively. Level 1 on Mars contains only two stardust effects—slowed movement and spinning—allowing players to adapt without becoming overwhelmed. The increasing quantity of the obstacles gradually raises the challenge, and the immediate feedback from collisions, such as sound effects and blinking animations helps players understand the consequences of their actions and adjust their strategy. When the astronaut collides with stardust, the escaping air bubbles from the leaky suit and the status bar change to match the colour of the stardust, providing immediate visual feedback that a status effect has been applied.

The game integrates the experience of Multiple Chemical Sensitivity (MCS) through the stardust mechanic. Each produces an unpredictable effect when it enters the astronaut's damaged suit, reflecting how environmental triggers can cause unexpected symptoms. In Level 1, the slowed movement represents fatigue, and the spinning represents dizziness. These effects persist for the remainder of the level, encouraging players to adapt to changing conditions, similarly to how individuals living with MCS must adapt to their symptoms, which can last for hours.

## Setup and Interaction Instructions

**Running the Sketch**
To run the sketch locally, open index.html in Google Chrome using Live Server.

**Game Objective**

Avoid obstacles and stardust, and reach your base before your oxygen runs out!

**Controls**

Movement:

- Up Arrow = Move Up
- Down Arrow = Move Down (while airborne)

Menu Controls:

- Mouse Click = Advance through mission instructions
- Mouse Click = Start mission / Continue mission / Restart mission

HUD Information:

- Air Supply: displayed in the top-left corner, shows remaining oxygen
- Warning from Mission Control: activates when oxygen becomes critically low
- Distance to Target: displayed across the bottom of the screen, indicates how far you are from reaching the base
- Win Condition: reach the base before your air supply reaches zero
- Lose Condition: your air supply reaches zero before reaching the base

Opening the Chrome Console:

- Windows: Press F12 or Ctrl + Shift + J, then click the Console tab
- Mac: Press Cmd + Option + J

The console will display any errors generated by the sketch
